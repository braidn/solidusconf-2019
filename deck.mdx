# Emotional Intelligence ?

> The capability of individuals to recognize their own emotions and those of others, 
  discern between different feelings and label them appropriately, 
  use emotional information to guide thinking and behavior, and manage and/or adjust emotions to adapt to environments or achieve one's goal(s)

<h1> 1. Self-Awareness </h1>
</Horizontal>

<Notes>
Self awareness shows itself in people where they understand their emotions. 
They also don't allow these emotions to control or direct them. 
Due to this, these humans tend to embody a lot of self confidence.

Due to all of this, these people tend to understand their own weakeness (and strengths). 
They work on them actively and better themselves consistently.
</Notes>

<h1>Self-Regulation</h1>
</Horizontal>

<Notes>
People who embody self-regulation are folks who think actively before they act. 
Due to this, they tend not to get too jealous or too angry. 
They control their emotions and often times appear none plussed most of the time.
</Notes>

<h1>Empathy</h1>
</Horizontal>

<Notes>
I have talked about this extensivelly in my previous two Solidus Conf talks.

Empathy is the (usually superhuman) ability to recognize the needs and emotions of the folks around you.
These people are quick to manage relations, listen, and relate to others around them. 
They almost always act in open and welcoming ways.

<H2>Structure</H2>
</Image>

<Notes>
We all know what a Monolith feels like when head first in one's development. 
We have worked in and around Solidus/Spree and Rails for the better half of a decade... 
And some of us even more.
Moving from this monolith to a microservices like 
structure isn't something that should be done because someone mentioned it on Medium.

Breaking up these beasts takes a concerted amount of consideration both, 
from the team perspective but, also from an architectural one.

Perhaps microservices isn't the first stop in this process, 
and maybe using our new EQ skills, we can discern if the team is ready for the move.
</Notes>
